Design Principles in Mobile Sites

Proximity — Austin Smith

BBC.com

BBC has great proximity because it has multiple boxes, they are all next to each other in an organized clean matter, and they accentuate the other boxes. It also has open spaces on the sides which help focus our attention to the middle. BBC also has a neat thin side bar at the top which also accentuate just how big and important the boxes are.

Alignment —Jacob Ottesen

GlassDoor.com

Design Explanation:
This website displays excellent alignment because of the text along the left side aligns perfectly with the search bar and the menu button in the right top corner.

Repetition — Justin Steele

npr.org

This website shows great use of repetition by the way it uses uniform boxes with stories and same text style all throughout the page. The screenshot image shows just the first few points of repetition where each story has bullet points that you can click to lead to other related stories and the text used here is all uniform. Each post has a picture in the same place, a title that has same text bolded and the small words above it that describe the general news it deals with, like politics, World, etc... The whole page uses repetition well and it enhances the page greatly.

Contrast — Heidi Winters

Netflix.com

Typography — Madison Allen

edstafford.org

This website is a good example of typography because the arrangement draws the eye naturally from top to bottom, and from left to right. The positioning of the homepage icon and sidebar icons is very deliberate, as to give the user the easiest time to navigate the web page. In addition, the banner image compliments the content and the web page as a whole as it ties everything together and naturally draws the eye to it. The content itself is centered and covered by a cyan box to emphasise itself and is the last piece of the website that the eye is naturally drawn to, completing the experience and overall making the website very, very tidy.
